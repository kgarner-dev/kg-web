Before I built anything, I asked a tattoo artist to walk me through her week.

Not the parts she posts on Instagram — the back-end. The part that doesn't have a highlight reel. How she takes a booking. How she sends consent forms. How she follows up with clients after a session. How she manages her flash availability. How she gets paid.

She pulled out her phone and started showing me. Instagram DMs for booking inquiries. A Google Form she'd copy-pasted from a Reddit thread for consent. A notes app for tracking deposits. Aftercare instructions she'd copy-pasted into the same three messages she sent every single client, every single time.

I asked her how long this took per client.

She thought about it. "Maybe two hours of admin for every appointment? And that's if nothing goes wrong."

She had 15 appointments that week.

That's 30 hours of work that had nothing to do with the actual work she's good at. Thirty hours spent inside a patchwork of tools that were never designed for her, stitched together because nothing better existed.

## What I Took Away

The problem wasn't that she needed a better app. She needed the entire workflow to disappear — to happen automatically, correctly, without her thinking about it. Booking confirmation, consent forms, pre-appointment reminders, aftercare instructions, healed photo follow-ups. All of it.

And she needed to own her client relationships. Not have them scattered across three platforms she doesn't control, one Instagram algorithm away from losing everything she'd built.

That conversation became the product spec for Stencled. Not a feature list — a commitment. Every artist gets a back-office that handles the administrative work and a public microsite that represents them professionally. The platform automates the pipeline from the moment a client books through long after the session is done.

And we never take a cut. Every dollar an artist earns is theirs.

That's not a pricing decision. It's the whole point.
